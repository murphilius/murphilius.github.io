About Me

I grew up in Kettering, Ohio. I went to Kettering Schools in the first grade. Around fourth or fifth grade, I realized that I love working with hardware (and bits of software). It was here that I saw how much I LOVE building computers and messing around with parts. By the time I was in eighth grade, I had heard about the CTC program for IT at Fairmont. It was then that I was pretty dead set on getting into the program.

Around the time that I was twelve, I had decided to start saving money for a computer. It took a lot of chores, a lot of birthday money, Christmas money, and even Easter money to finally save up enough for my dream project. By the time I was thirteen, I could see the light at the end of the tunnel. My plan finally started by the time summer started. I bought certain parts off of Amazon, NewEgg, and even went an hour away to Micro Center just to get something I had set my mind on. I had gotten all the parts by the time the summer ended, and I was ecstatic.

Around the time that I had got a job and started saving money for a few months, I had gotten to experience wiring ethernet cables throughout the house. It was a nice project to see how much ethernet cable I might need for a distance, and it was fun to use my brain to get the cables through the ceilings. Now that I had a substantially better internet connection, I was able to relax and play the casual video games I enjoy with my friends.
